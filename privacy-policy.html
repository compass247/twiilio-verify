1. Introduction

Compass Medical Admin Services ("CMAS," "we," "our," or "us") provides AI-powered medical administrative services to healthcare providers, including clinical note entry, medical coding, charge capture, and clinic message handling.

This Privacy Policy describes how we collect, use, disclose, and protect your information when you use our services, website, and platform (collectively, the "Services"). We are committed to protecting the privacy and security of all information entrusted to us, including Protected Health Information (PHI) as defined by HIPAA.

HIPAA Notice: CMAS operates as a Business Associate under HIPAA. All PHI we process on behalf of healthcare providers (Covered Entities) is governed by our Business Associate Agreement (BAA) and applicable HIPAA regulations.

2. Information We Collect

2.1 Protected Health Information (PHI)

In the course of providing Services to healthcare providers, we may process:

  • Patient names, dates of birth, and demographic information
  • Clinical notes, SOAP notes, and medical records
  • Diagnosis codes (ICD-10) and procedure codes (CPT)
  • Prescription and medication information
  • Lab results, imaging reports, and referral documents
  • Insurance and billing information
  • Clinical messages (patient-provider communications)

2.2 Account Information

When healthcare providers register for our Services, we collect:

  • Provider name, credentials, and NPI number
  • Practice name, address, and contact information
  • Email address and login credentials
  • Billing and payment information

2.3 Usage and Technical Data

  • IP address and device information
  • Browser type and operating system
  • Pages visited and features used
  • Service usage patterns and analytics
  • Audit logs (access timestamps, actions performed)

3. How We Use Your Information

We use the information we collect to:

  • Provide Services: Process clinical notes, generate billing codes, handle clinic messages, and deliver medical admin support
  • AI Processing: Use artificial intelligence to transcribe, code, and draft clinical documents for provider review and approval
  • Quality Assurance: Monitor accuracy, conduct random QA checks, and improve AI performance
  • Compliance: Maintain audit trails, meet HIPAA requirements, and respond to legal obligations
  • Communication: Send service notifications, respond to support requests, and share updates
  • Improvement: Analyze usage patterns to enhance our Services (using de-identified data only)
Important: We never sell PHI or personal health data. We do not use PHI for marketing purposes. AI-generated outputs are always subject to physician review and approval before finalization.

4. HIPAA Compliance

CMAS complies with the Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act (HIPAA), including:

  • Business Associate Agreement (BAA): We execute a BAA with each healthcare provider (Covered Entity) before processing any PHI
  • Privacy Rule: We use and disclose PHI only as permitted by the BAA and HIPAA regulations
  • Security Rule: We implement administrative, physical, and technical safeguards to protect ePHI
  • Breach Notification Rule: We notify affected Covered Entities within 24 hours of discovering a breach of unsecured PHI
  • Minimum Necessary Standard: We limit PHI access to the minimum necessary to perform our Services

5. Data Security

We implement comprehensive security measures to protect your data:

  • Encryption: All data encrypted in transit (TLS 1.2+) and at rest (AES-256 / SSE-S3)
  • Access Control: Role-based access, multi-factor authentication (MFA), and least-privilege principles
  • Audit Logging: AWS CloudTrail monitors and logs all system activities for 6 years (per HIPAA requirements)
  • Infrastructure: Hosted on AWS with signed BAA, using HIPAA-eligible services only
  • Network Security: VPC isolation, firewall rules, DDoS protection via Cloudflare
  • Employee Training: All team members complete HIPAA training and sign confidentiality agreements
  • Incident Response: Documented incident response plan with defined escalation procedures

6. Third-Party Services

We use the following third-party services to deliver our platform. Each processes data under a BAA or equivalent data protection agreement:

  • Amazon Web Services (AWS): Cloud infrastructure, AI/ML processing (Bedrock), data storage — BAA signed
  • Twilio: Communication services (SMS/voice notifications) — HIPAA BAA signed
  • Cloudflare: DNS, CDN, and DDoS protection
  • Google Workspace: Business email and internal collaboration

We do not share PHI with any third party except as necessary to provide our Services, as required by law, or as authorized by the applicable Covered Entity.

7. Data Retention

  • PHI and audit logs: Retained for a minimum of 6 years as required by HIPAA (45 CFR §164.530(j))
  • Account information: Retained for the duration of the service relationship plus 6 years
  • Usage analytics: Retained in de-identified form for up to 3 years
  • After retention period: Data is securely deleted using industry-standard methods

8. Your Rights

For Healthcare Providers (Our Clients)

  • Access and export your account data at any time
  • Request correction of inaccurate account information
  • Terminate your account and request data deletion (subject to retention requirements)
  • Receive breach notification as specified in the BAA

For Patients

Patient rights regarding PHI are governed by the relationship between the patient and their healthcare provider (Covered Entity). Patients should contact their healthcare provider directly to exercise HIPAA rights, including:

  • Right to access their medical records
  • Right to request amendment of their records
  • Right to an accounting of disclosures
  • Right to request restrictions on use/disclosure

California Residents (CCPA/CPRA)

California residents may have additional rights under the California Consumer Privacy Act. Note that PHI governed by HIPAA is exempt from CCPA. For non-PHI personal information, you may request disclosure, deletion, or opt-out of sale (we do not sell personal information).

9. Children's Privacy

Our Services are designed for healthcare providers and are not directed at individuals under 18. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children. PHI of minor patients is processed solely under the direction of the Covered Entity in accordance with HIPAA.

10. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. When we make material changes, we will:

  • Update the "Last Updated" date at the top of this page
  • Notify registered users via email
  • Post a notice on our website

Continued use of our Services after changes constitutes acceptance of the updated policy.
